Units of measurement

The following tables list typical units of measurement to use when specifying ingredients or items for a recipe page or a shopping list page.

When you want to provide more than one way to measure an item, you can provide an array of units using the Measurement object. For example, you can specify one liter or 32 fluid ounces for chicken broth.

Measured Items
cup, cups, or c	walnuts, heavy cream, rolled oats
fl oz	tomato soup, sweet corn, olive oil, rice vinegar
gallon, gallons, gal, or gals	whole milk, distilled water
milliliter, millilitre, milliliters, millilitres, ml, or mls	milk, apple juice
liter, litre, liters, litres, or l	alkaline water, passion fruit juice
pint, pints, pt, or pts	ice cream
pt container	blueberries
quart, quarts, qt, or qts	ice cream
tablespoon, tablespoons, tb, or tbs	olive oil, salt, sugar
teaspoon, teaspoons, ts, tsp, or tspn	pepper, curry, cinnamon


Weighed Items
gram, grams, g, or gs	rice noodles
kilogram, kilograms, kg, or kgs	chicken, beef, pork
lb bag or lb	potatoes, grapefruit, grapes
lb can or lb	shredded chicken
lb container or lb	strawberries, blackberries
per lb	pears, tangelos, apples
ounce, ounces, or oz	cereal, butter, oats
ounces bag or oz bag	dog treats, cat treats, pineapple chunks
ounces can or oz can	canned black beans, canned tuna
ounces container or oz container	turkey quick meal, greek salad meal
pound, pounds, lb, or lbs	beef, flour, pork

TIP For countable items in a recipe, such as tomatoes, use the unit "each" rather than specifying a weight.
